Our Mission

Why we exist.

The Ghana AI Literacy Project exists to bridge the artificial intelligence knowledge gap across Ghana by equipping young people, educators, and underserved communities with the skills, tools, and confidence to participate fully in the global digital economy.

We believe that access to quality AI education is not a privilege — it is a right. Through hands-on workshops and partnerships with schools, we are building a generation of Ghanaian innovators who are not just consumers of technology, but creators of it.

The people who do not know how to use AI are already being left behind — not because they are less capable, but because they never had access. That is exactly what this project is here to fix.

"Everyone should have a chance. Not just those who are rich, not just students in Silicon Valley, London, or New York."

— Prince Kwarteng, Founder

Our Vision

A Ghana — and an Africa — where no young person is excluded from the opportunities that artificial intelligence presents, and where the next generation of global AI leaders speaks with an African voice.

Access
Bringing AI education into communities and schools regardless of resources.
Equity
Prioritizing girls, rural youth, and communities most at risk of being left behind.
Relevance
Teaching AI through the lens of Ghanaian culture and real local challenges.
Empowerment
Developing confident thinkers who shape how AI is used across Africa.

About the Founder

Prince Kwarteng

"Everyone should have a chance. Not just those who are rich, not just students in Silicon Valley, London, or New York."

My name is Prince Kwarteng. I am a student at Madison Country Day School in Wisconsin, and I am Ghanaian-Canadian. I did not come here to visit. I came here to change something.

When I look at how fast the world is moving with artificial intelligence, I see a gap that nobody should fall into simply because of where they were born. These young people are not behind. They have never had access. There is a difference. And that difference is exactly what this project is here to fix.

Everyone deserves a seat at the table. I am just making sure Ghana pulls up a chair.

The Curriculum

5 days. Real skills.

Each day builds on the last — from understanding what AI is, to using it confidently, to thinking critically about its impact on their world.

Day 01
Introduction to AI
What is AI? Where is it already in your life? Students discover that AI is not distant — it is already here.
Day 02
How AI Learns
Demystifying machine learning. Data, patterns, and how computers get smarter over time.
Day 03
AI Tools in Practice
Hands-on: students use real AI tools and see what they can create, solve, and explore.
Day 04
Ethics & Bias
Who builds AI? Who does it serve? Students think critically about fairness, power, and representation.
Day 05
Your AI Future
Students imagine and present: how could AI solve a real problem in their community?
